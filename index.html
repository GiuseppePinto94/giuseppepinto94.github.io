C​oordinated energy management OF  BUILDINGS WITH ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

When building energy management is performed at scale, uncoordinated strategies may have negative effects on the grid reliability. To overcome these limitations, the paper exploits AI to take advantage of the mutual collaboration between single buildings,  pursuing a coordinated approach.

Scaling Building Energy management with surrogate models 

Scaling energy management while not losing bottom-level information is crucial in smart grids. The proposed methodology makes use of a fully data-driven control scheme which exploits LSTM to simulate indoor building dynamics and DRL to optimize energy consumption of multiple buildings.  

READ MORE 

TRANSFER LEARNING IN SMART BUILDINGS

Transfer learning may help to unleash untapped potential of advanced analytics and control strategies in smart buildings. ​The present work provides a comprehensive overview of transfer learning applications in smart buildings, discussing research gaps and future directions.

READ MORE 

Multi-agent architectures for district energy management

Two multi-agent reinforcement learning methods are explored: a centralised (coordinated) controller and a decentralised (cooperative) controller, which are benchmarked against a rule-based controller. The study shows that the multi-agent cooperative approach may be more suitable for districts with heterogeneous objectives within the individual buildings.
